# Clickzolo Docs

Clickzolo Docs is a public knowledge hub for advertisers, agencies, and in-house growth teams that want to understand click fraud, traffic quality, invalid clicks, and Google Ads budget protection more clearly.

If you want to explore the live product, visit [Clickzolo](https://clickzolo.com/).

## What you will find here

- practical explanations of click fraud in Google Ads
- guidance on identifying suspicious paid traffic patterns
- educational notes on invalid clicks and refund evidence
- industry-specific traffic quality considerations
- comparison and buyer-research style content around click fraud protection tools

## Who this is for

- Google Ads advertisers trying to reduce wasted spend
- agencies managing multiple paid traffic accounts
- ecommerce teams protecting performance margin
- lead generation teams focused on traffic quality
- buyers comparing click fraud protection platforms

## Key topics

### Click fraud basics

Learn what click fraud means in practice, how it appears in paid search campaigns, and why wasted clicks damage both budget and decision-making quality.

### Invalid clicks and refund evidence

Understand what advertisers usually document when they review suspicious traffic and how cleaner evidence supports smarter follow-up.

### Traffic quality monitoring

See the kinds of patterns teams watch when they want to separate real buyer intent from repeated low-value activity.

### Industry use cases

Different businesses feel traffic-quality problems differently. Ecommerce, lead gen, local services, and agency-managed accounts often care about different signals.

### Product research

This repo can also support neutral, educational comparison content for teams researching solutions in the click fraud protection category.

## Why this matters

Bad traffic does more than waste ad spend. It can distort campaign learning, weaken conversion data, and make it harder to understand which audiences, keywords, and creatives are actually working.

That is why more teams are treating traffic quality as an ongoing operational concern instead of a one-time cleanup task.
